# Narration script — 4Line On-Chain (EN)

_Ready to read over the recorded footage (OBS sped up 4–8x). Target: 2:30–3:00.
Each block = one scene from the recording. Read at pace, pausing between blocks._

---

### 1 · Open (hero)
> "Every World Cup fan watches with a phone in hand. **4Line On-Chain** turns that into a
> **provably-fair** pool: every pick is a meme on top, but a real **TxLINE oracle** market
> underneath. Nobody — not even the house — can fake the result."

### 2 · Building the ticket
> "I build a ticket in seconds. First the **result lock** — the 1X2. Miss it and the whole
> ticket zeroes, so it rewards reading the game, not luck. Then the meme markets by sector:
> **team stats**, **star players** — powered by the oracle's PlayerStats — and **fun bonuses**.
> The harder the call, the more it pays."

### 3 · Seal → pick pools → single payment
> "When I seal, I choose **which pools to enter** — and pay for all of them in a **single
> charge**. Here: two pools, a hundred and fifty reais combined. And notice — pools for a
> different match show up **locked**. A ticket only enters where it's compatible."

### 4 · Create a pool (the rules)
> "Creating a pool is the heart of the product. I set the **matches**, the **buy-in**,
> **one or many tickets per person** — with a warning: the highest-scoring ticket wins, the
> rest are eliminated — and the **scoring mode**: by points, or result-only with the prize
> split among everyone who's right. This is exactly the track's **'Group Sweepstake'** idea —
> except the **leaderboard updates itself from TxLINE**. No spreadsheet, no waiting."

### 5 · Pool panel (ranking, prizes, invite)
> "Every pool has its own panel: **prizes**, **rules**, and the **live leaderboard** with the
> members. And you bring your friends in with an **invite link** — whoever opens it joins the
> same pool."

### 6 · Provably-fair (the real settlement)
> "Now the serious part. Every ticket **commits a hash** before kickoff. The result arrives
> from the **TxLINE oracle**, and each pick is settled: right ✓, wrong ✗. Here the lock hit,
> and the ticket scored. But **this** one — the lock missed, and the ticket **zeroed**. Miss
> the result, and nothing counts. That's the heart of the mechanic — honest and verifiable."

### 7 · Close
> "Final score, straight from the oracle. On the roadmap: a **Telegram bot** with your bet's
> live score, a real cross-user backend, and **on-chain** settlement. 4Line On-Chain is live
> at **4line-onchain.vercel.app**, open source on GitHub. Built by a fan, for fans."
